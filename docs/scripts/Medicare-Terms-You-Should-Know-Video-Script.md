**VISUAL:** Piggy bank filling up + calendar showing beginning of year

### **[0-8 seconds] - HOOK**

---

### **[9-32 seconds] - TERM 1: DEDUCTIBLE**
**VISUAL:** Calendar showing January 1st + hospital building + wallet opening  
**TEXT OVERLAY:** 
- "Term #1: DEDUCTIBLE"
- "Amount YOU pay BEFORE insurance helps"
- "Medicare Part A: $1,676 per hospital stay"
- "Medicare Part B: $257 per year"
- "Drug Plans: $200-$500 typically"
- "Resets every January 1st"

**VOICEOVER:**  
*"Term one: Deductible. This is what you must pay out of pocket before your insurance starts covering anything. Original Medicare Part A - that's hospital coverage - has a $1,676 deductible per benefit period. Part B has a $257 annual deductible. Drug plans have their own deductibles, usually $200 to $500. And here's the kicker: it resets every January 1st. Every year, you start over."*

---

### **[33-57 seconds] - TERM 2: COPAY**
**VISUAL:** Doctor's office + prescription counter + specific dollar amounts appearing  
**TEXT OVERLAY:** 
- "Term #2: COPAY"
- "FIXED dollar amount per service"
- "Primary Care: $0-$25"
- "Specialist: $30-$50"
- "Generic Drug: $5-$10"
- "Brand Drug: $40-$100"
- "Same amount every time"

**VOICEOVER:**  
*"Term two: Copay, short for co-payment. This is a fixed dollar amount you pay each time you use a service. See your primary care doctor? Maybe $20. Specialist? Could be $40. Pick up a generic prescription? Ten bucks. The key word is FIXED. You know exactly what you'll pay every time. Makes budgeting easier. Medicare Advantage plans typically use copays more than Original Medicare."*

---

### **[58-80 seconds] - TERM 3: COINSURANCE**
**VISUAL:** Calculator + 80/20 split animation + real medical bill example  
**TEXT OVERLAY:** 
- "Term #3: COINSURANCE"
- "You pay a PERCENTAGE of the cost"
- "Original Medicare: 80/20 split"
- "Example 1: $1,000 test = $200 you pay"
- "Example 2: $10,000 surgery = $2,000 you pay"
- "No limit on Original Medicare!"

**VOICEOVER:**  
*"Term three: Coinsurance. This is a percentage you're responsible for. Original Medicare typically covers 80%, you pay 20%. Sounds simple, but watch out: if your MRI costs $1,000, you pay $200. If your hip surgery costs $10,000, you pay $2,000. And with Original Medicare, there's no out-of-pocket maximum. That 20% can add up fast. This is why many people add a Medigap plan - to cover that coinsurance."*

- "COPAY: Fixed $ per visit"
- "COINSURANCE: % of total cost"
- "Know YOUR costs: YourMedGuy.com"

**VOICEOVER:**  
*"So remember: Deductible is what you pay first. Copay is a fixed amount. Coinsurance is a percentage. Different plans use these differently. Comment GUIDE for our cost calculator, or book a consultation at YourMedGuy.com to see your actual costs."*

## **ACCESSIBILITY & COMPLIANCE:**

### **Accessibility:**
- Large, high-contrast numbers
- Full captions with mathematical symbols
- Slow enough pacing for note-taking
- Repeat key numbers for emphasis
- Screen reader friendly descriptions

### **Compliance Language:**

**In Description:**
*"Costs shown are for 2026 and may vary by plan and location. For personalized cost analysis, consult a licensed insurance agent. We represent multiple insurance carriers and can help you compare options."*

**Avoid:**
- Guaranteeing specific costs
- "This plan is always cheaper"
- Advising without knowing full situation
- Showing real client bills without permission
