### **Common Follow-Up Questions & Responses:**

**Q: "Does the Donut Hole reset every year?"**
A: "YES! Every January 1st you start over at Phase 1 (deductible). If you hit catastrophic coverage in November, you'll drop back to deductible phase on Jan 1. This is important for budgeting!"

**Q: "Do all Part D plans have a Donut Hole?"**
A: "Most do, but some plans offer 'gap coverage' - they cover your costs IN the Donut Hole. These plans have higher premiums but can save money if you have expensive drugs. Want me to show you which plans have gap coverage?"

**Q: "What if I hit the Donut Hole in December?"**
A: "Then it resets in January anyway! You won't hit catastrophic coverage. This is why TIMING matters. If you'll hit it late in the year, it might not be worth paying for gap coverage."

**Q: "Do vaccines count toward the Donut Hole?"**
A: "Great question! Part B vaccines (flu, pneumonia, shingles) DON'T count - they're covered differently. But Part D medications DO count toward pushing you through the phases."

**Q: "What's considered 'catastrophic coverage'?"**
A: "After you've spent about $8,000 total on drugs in a year, you pay either $4.50 for generics or $11.20 for brand names, OR 5% of the cost - whichever is greater. Basically almost nothing!"

**Q: "Can I change plans if I realize I'll hit the Donut Hole?"**
A: "Only during Annual Enrollment Period (Oct 15-Dec 7) for next year, or if you qualify for a Special Enrollment Period. This is why calculating BEFORE enrolling is so important!"
