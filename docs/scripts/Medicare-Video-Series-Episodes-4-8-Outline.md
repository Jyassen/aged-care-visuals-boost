## **EPISODE 4: "Out-of-Pocket Maximum Explained"**

### **Core Concept:**
Explain what an out-of-pocket maximum is, why it matters, and the HUGE difference between plans that have one vs. don't.

### **60-Second Script Outline:**

**Hook (0-7s):**  
*"The most important Medicare number nobody tells you about..."*

**Main Content (8-50s):**
- **What is it:** The MOST you'll pay in a year, then insurance covers 100%
- **Who has it:** Medicare Advantage plans ($8,000-$10,000 typical)
- **Who DOESN'T:** Original Medicare (NO CAP on that 20%!)
- **Real example:** $100,000 cancer treatment
  - Medicare Advantage: You pay $8,000 max
  - Original Medicare: You pay $20,000+ (no limit!)
- **Why it matters:** Protects you from catastrophic costs

**CTA (51-60s):**  
*"This one number can save you tens of thousands. Comment GUIDE for plan comparison or book at YourMedGuy.com"*

### **Key Visual:**
- Meter filling up to maximum, then stops (Advantage)
- Meter filling up with no limit, breaking through ceiling (Original Medicare)
- Side-by-side cost comparison for expensive procedure

### **Why This Episode:**
- Most people don't know Original Medicare has NO out-of-pocket maximum
- This is the #1 reason people get Medigap or choose Advantage
- Creates urgency around plan selection
- High shareability ("Did you know...?")

## **EPISODE 5: "The Donut Hole Demystified"**

### **Core Concept:**
Break down the confusing Medicare Part D "coverage gap" (donut hole) in simple terms with real examples.

### **60-Second Script Outline:**

**Hook (0-7s):**  
*"Why is it called the Medicare Donut Hole? And why does it cost you more money?"*

**Main Content (8-50s):**
- **Visual:** Donut graphic with hole in middle
- **Phase 1 - Deductible:** You pay first ~$500
- **Phase 2 - Initial Coverage:** You pay copay/coinsurance (good!)
- **Phase 3 - DONUT HOLE:** Once you hit ~$5,000 total drug costs
  - You pay 25% of brand name drugs
  - You pay 25% of generic drugs
  - This is MORE than before!
- **Phase 4 - Catastrophic:** After $8,000 you pay almost nothing
- **Real example:** Someone on expensive meds hitting donut hole in June, paying $200/month more until December

**CTA (51-60s):**  
*"If you take expensive medications, this matters. Comment GUIDE for drug cost calculator or book at YourMedGuy.com"*

### **Key Visual:**
- Animated donut with 4 phases clearly marked
- Dollar amounts at each phase
- Timeline showing when typical person hits each phase
- Comparison: Plan with donut hole vs. plan without

### **Why This Episode:**
- Donut hole confuses everyone
- Critical for people on multiple/expensive medications
- Shows expertise (most agents don't explain this well)
- Leads naturally to Part D plan comparison tool

## **EPISODE 8: "Premium vs Total Cost: What Really Matters"**

### **Core Concept:**
Explain why choosing based on lowest premium is often the most expensive mistake.

### **60-Second Script Outline:**

**Hook (0-7s):**  
*"A $0 premium plan cost Sarah $8,000 more than a $200/month plan. Here's how..."*

**Main Content (8-50s):**

**The Trap:** Everyone shops for lowest premium
- $0 premium plan looks amazing!
- But premium is just ONE cost

**Real Example - Plan A vs Plan B:**

**Plan A: $0 Premium**
- Deductible: $500
- PCP Copay: $40
- Specialist: $80
- Hospital: $500/day
- **Sarah's annual cost:** $8,740

**Plan B: $200/month Premium**
- Deductible: $0
- PCP Copay: $10
- Specialist: $30
- Hospital: $250/day
- **Sarah's annual cost:** $5,400

**The Math:** Plan A looks free but costs $3,340 MORE per year!

**CTA (51-60s):**  
*"Total cost is what matters. Calculate yours: Comment GUIDE or YourMedGuy.com"*

### **Key Visual:**
- Two plan cards side-by-side
- Premium highlighted on each
- Then reveal total costs below
- Bar graph showing premium vs total cost
- "Don't fall for the $0 trap" warning

### **Extended 90-Second Version Additions:**
- Show 3-4 different health scenarios:
  - Healthy person who rarely goes to doctor
  - Person with chronic condition
  - Someone planning surgery
  - Frequent prescription user
- Best plan changes based on YOUR situation
- When $0 premium DOES make sense vs when it doesn't

### **Why This Episode:**
- Addresses #1 consumer mistake
- Creates "aha moment" 
- Differentiates from agents who just show premiums
- Natural lead to consultation ("Let me help you calculate")
