**Q: "Why don't agents tell you about Part B premium?"**
A: "Because they don't get paid on Part B (it's government, not insurance). They focus on what they sell - the Advantage or Supplement plans. But YOU need to know your total cost! That's why we show everything."

**Q: "Can I avoid the Part B premium?"**
A: "No, everyone on Medicare pays it (unless you qualify for financial assistance). But high earners can sometimes reduce it by managing their income in the years before 65. Talk to a financial advisor about IRMAA planning."

**Q: "How do I check if a doctor is in-network?"**
A: "Call the doctor's office AND the insurance plan. Get it in writing if possible. For surgery, ask the hospital about ALL providers (surgeon, anesthesiologist, pathologist, radiologist). This prevents surprise bills!"

**Q: "Do any plans cover hearing aids?"**
A: "Some Medicare Advantage plans include hearing aid benefits ($500-$2,000 allowance typically). Original Medicare does NOT. This is one advantage of MA plans. Want me to show you which plans offer the best hearing coverage?"

**Q: "Is the Part B premium the same in every state?"**
A: "YES! Part B premium is federal - same nationwide. But Advantage/Supplement plan premiums vary by location. That's why local expertise matters."
