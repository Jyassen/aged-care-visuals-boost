### üé® VISUAL STYLE GUIDELINES

#### Photography Processing:
- **Color Grading:** Warm tones (slight orange/yellow tint)
- **Contrast:** Medium (not too harsh)
- **Saturation:** Natural to slightly enhanced
- **Lighting:** Soft, diffused (avoid harsh shadows)
- **Filters:** None or very subtle (maintain authenticity)

#### Composition Rules:
- **Rule of Thirds:** Position subjects off-center
- **Leading Lines:** Use environmental elements to guide eye to subject
- **Depth of Field:** Soft background blur to highlight subjects
- **White Space:** Allow breathing room around subjects
- **Eye Level:** Shoot at subject's eye level (not looking down)

## Content Creation Guidelines

### DIY Photography (Low Budget Option)

#### Equipment Needed:
- Modern smartphone (iPhone 13+, Samsung S21+)
- Natural lighting (shoot near windows)
- Simple background (home, park, office)
- Willing participants (clients with permission releases)

#### Shooting Tips:
1. **Lighting:** Shoot in morning or late afternoon (golden hour)
2. **Background:** Keep simple and uncluttered
3. **Composition:** Use portrait mode for background blur
4. **Angles:** Shoot at eye level, not from above
5. **Multiple Shots:** Take 50+ photos to get 10 great ones
6. **Candid Moments:** Capture natural interactions

## Mobile Optimization Notes

### Image Considerations for Mobile:

1. **Hero Images:**
   - Provide mobile-specific crop (vertical focus)
   - Key subject should be in center-top of frame
   - Text overlay must remain readable
   - Face/emotion should be visible even at small size

2. **Section Images:**
   - May need to switch from horizontal to square on mobile
   - Consider stacking instead of side-by-side
   - Images should work at 375px wide minimum

3. **File Size:**
   - Desktop: Max 300KB per image
   - Mobile: Max 150KB per image
   - Use WebP format for 30% smaller files
   - Lazy loading for below-fold images

## Accessibility Considerations

### Visual Design Must Include:

1. **Alt Text for All Images:**
   - Descriptive (not just "senior" but "senior woman reviewing Medicare documents with agent in bright kitchen")
   - Emotion included ("smiling senior couple")

2. **Color Contrast:**
   - Text over images must meet WCAG AA standards (4.5:1)
   - Use semi-transparent overlays behind text
   - Test with contrast checker tools

3. **Image Captions:**
   - For key images, add visible captions
   - Especially for testimonials and team photos

4. **Loading States:**
   - Skeleton screens while images load
   - Progressive image loading
   - Text-only fallback if images fail

## Red Flags to Avoid

### ‚ùå What NOT To Do:

1. **Overly Clinical Imagery:**
   - No hospital beds
   - No IV drips or medical equipment
   - No emphasis on illness/disease

2. **Depressing Aging Stereotypes:**
   - No wheelchairs unless specifically relevant
   - No nursing home settings
   - No "lonely senior" imagery

3. **Fake Stock Photo Red Flags:**
   - Perfect white teeth on seniors (unrealistic)
   - Models that look like models (not real people)
   - Overly diverse group shots (rainbow coalition)
   - Studio lighting with harsh shadows

4. **Confusing Imagery:**
   - Abstract concepts (puzzle pieces, etc.)
   - Metaphorical images (journey/path without people)
   - Tech-heavy (circuit boards, data visualizations)

5. **Cultural Insensitivity:**
   - Assuming all seniors are the same
   - Ignoring cultural differences in health/family
   - Stereotypical representations
